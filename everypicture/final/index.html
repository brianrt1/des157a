The House

Kings Beach, CA

Beginning at an abandoned structure, Line + Form exists all around us. This structure uses sheet metal which are corrugated, giving the building a repeating line pattern wrapping around everything. This is enboldened by the roof-line, which serves as a guiding line and reveals the true structure of building. The lamp's unique shape overhangs the front facade, breaking away from the hard angles and materials.

The Tower

Sacramento, CA

Engineering is not conceptually beautiful, the radiating lines converging shapes give this tower a signature design. A symphony of hard angles and organic form, this atrium celebrates it's construction by using raw form. In this case, the steel structure supports the glass facade but also serves as a sculptural statement.

Deathstar

Davis, CA

This modern buidling is a reflection of raw form and materials, unfolding at endless, arbitrary angles. The flat faces and heavy use of concrete forces us to give in to the forms, which are offputing and uneasy. The sharp angles of this building is a drift from the green and suburb surroundings of the building. The layout is a testament of organic form using sharp, unpredictable shapes.

The Tracks

Davis, CA

Representing long connections, the railroad is a stretch of line and form. The repeating tracks cut through landscapes, cities, and along mountainsides. Rails are structures that have standards, they must be made of specific materials and repeating wooden planks are often used as support. The rails create parallel lines over the landscape that travel along side eachother but will never converge.

The Entry

Sacramento, CA

This utopian like entry way is formed by a second-level walkway and a sloped platform down to the ground level. This building is integrated into nature, complete with a roof-top green space. The entry is intuitive. Located on a street corner, the form of the entry works as an enterance to an arena. The sloping walkways goes lower than the sidewalk, implying that you're entering a different space. The repeating lines of the building behind the complex give a look into what lies ahead.

The Structure

Sacramento, CA

A building without a facade reveals the true nature of a strcuture. The grid-like colums all point to the univerality of buiding techniques. Based on this form, the building could be an office, a hotel, or an apartment building. Without a facade, we're left only with the part which actaully makes the building stand, the structure. The beams stretch from the ground floor to the tallest point, going across too on every floor. You can also see triangles made from the cross sections of the beams.
